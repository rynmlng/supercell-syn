**Concerns:**
- Surface-based CAPE is modest (186-292 J/kg) — may limit updraft intensity
- CINH is initially strong (-282 J/kg SFC) — delayed initiation possible
- LCL at 1532m is moderate — not ideal for tornado efficiency (prefer <1000m)

**Bottom line:** A classic southern Plains spring severe weather setup with a well-defined dry line and excellent wind shear. The modest CAPE is the main limiter, but the outstanding low-level shear (SRH 176) compensates significantly. Discrete supercells at the tail end of the developing QLCS in south-central Kansas are the prime targets, with tornado potential if cells can tap the richer moisture east of the dry line.
